Luna

Earth's only natural satellite, the Moon, is a barren and cratered world bathed in the constant light of the sun. Its dusty, gray surface is marked by the scars of ancient impacts, and its thin atmosphere offers no protection from the harshness of space. Yet, this celestial body has long held a special place in human imagination, inspiring myths, stories, and dreams for millennia.

Moon Phases

New Moon

Darkness reigns as the moon hides behind Earth's shadow, cloaking the night in an inky veil.

Waxing Crescent

A shy sliver of light graces the western sky, marking the moon's subtle return to our celestial dance.

First Quarter

A perfectly halved moon stands proud, a luminous sentinel guarding the midnight hour.

Waxing Gibbous

Bathed in ever-growing sunlight, the moon swells with anticipation, its luminous form plump and inviting.

Full Moon

A radiant orb bathes the world in its silvery glow, casting long, ethereal shadows and igniting the night with its majestic presence.

Waning Gibbous

Reluctantly surrendering its brilliance, the moon wanes, its luminous curves surrendering to the encroaching darkness.

Third Quarter

A stoic half-moon bids farewell, its remaining light a silent testament to the ever-turning celestial wheel.

Waning Crescent

A final, fading whisper of light lingers in the western sky, a fleeting promise of the moon's imminent return.
